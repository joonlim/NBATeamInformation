<p>The <b>2014–15 NBA season</b> is the 69th season of the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA). The <a href="/wiki/2014_NBA_draft" title="2014 NBA draft">NBA draft</a> took place on June 26, 2014, at <a href="/wiki/Barclays_Center" title="Barclays Center">Barclays Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Wiggins" title="Andrew Wiggins">Andrew Wiggins</a> was selected first overall. The regular season began on Tuesday, October 28, 2014, when the defending NBA champions <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Spurs" title="San Antonio Spurs">San Antonio Spurs</a> hosted the first game against the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>. <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association_Christmas_games" title="National Basketball Association Christmas games">Christmas games</a> will be played on Thursday, December 25, 2014. The <a href="/wiki/2015_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="2015 NBA All-Star Game">2015 NBA All-Star Game</a> will be played on Sunday, February 15, 2015, at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. The regular season will end on Wednesday, April 15, 2015, and the playoffs will begin on Saturday, April 18, 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transactions">Transactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Transactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season_transactions" title="List of 2014–15 NBA season transactions">List of 2014–15 NBA season transactions</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>On June 10, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Fisher" title="Derek Fisher">Derek Fisher</a> signed a five-year, $25 million deal as the head coach of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a>, instead of playing one more season with the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_Thunder" title="Oklahoma City Thunder">Oklahoma City Thunder</a>. During his eighteen seasons in the league as a player, he won five NBA championships, all with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a> (2000–2002 and 2009–2010).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>On June 15, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Shane_Battier" title="Shane Battier">Shane Battier</a> announced his retirement immediately following the loss of Game 5 in the <a href="/wiki/2014_NBA_Finals" title="2014 NBA Finals">2014 NBA Finals</a>. He was 35, and had played for thirteen seasons. He won back-to-back NBA championships with the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Heat" title="Miami Heat">Miami Heat</a> (2012–2013)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>On September 9, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Chauncey_Billups" title="Chauncey Billups">Chauncey Billups</a> announced retirement after 17 years of playing; the last team he played for was the Detroit Pistons, and he won a championship with them in 2004 as well as a Finals MVP that same year.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_agency">Free agency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free agency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/NBA_free_agency" title="NBA free agency" class="mw-redirect">Free agency</a> negotiations began on July 1, 2014, when players began to sign starting on July 10, after the <a href="/wiki/July_moratorium" title="July moratorium" class="mw-redirect">July moratorium</a> ended.</p>
<ul>
<li>On June 23, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a> announced that <a href="/wiki/Carmelo_Anthony" title="Carmelo Anthony">Carmelo Anthony</a> will try free agency.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On June 23, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Heat" title="Miami Heat">Miami Heat</a> announced that <a href="/wiki/LeBron_James" title="LeBron James">LeBron James</a> will become a free agent.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>In late June 2014, <a href="/wiki/Dwyane_Wade" title="Dwyane Wade">Dwyane Wade</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Bosh" title="Chris Bosh">Chris Bosh</a> opted out of their contracts with the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Heat" title="Miami Heat">Miami Heat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On July 11, 2014, <a href="/wiki/LeBron_James" title="LeBron James">LeBron James</a> announced that he will return to the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On July 12, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Pau_Gasol" title="Pau Gasol">Pau Gasol</a> reported joining the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bulls" title="Chicago Bulls">Chicago Bulls</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On July 12, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Thomas_(basketball,_born_1989)" title="Isaiah Thomas (basketball, born 1989)">Isaiah Thomas</a> signed with the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a> in sign and trade.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On July 12, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Bosh" title="Chris Bosh">Chris Bosh</a> re-signed with the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Heat" title="Miami Heat">Miami Heat</a> for a five year, $118 million contract.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On July 13, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Carmelo_Anthony" title="Carmelo Anthony">Carmelo Anthony</a> announced that he will return to <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On July 13, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Pierce" title="Paul Pierce">Paul Pierce</a> signed with the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Wizards" title="Washington Wizards">Washington Wizards</a> for a two year, $11 million contract.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On July 15, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Dwyane_Wade" title="Dwyane Wade">Dwyane Wade</a> re-signed with the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Heat" title="Miami Heat">Miami Heat</a> for a 2 year, $34 million contract.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Offseason">Offseason</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Offseason">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>On April 21, 2014, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a> fired head coach <a href="/wiki/Mike_Woodson" title="Mike Woodson">Mike Woodson</a> after two years with the team.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On April 21, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Minnesota Timberwolves</a> head coach <a href="/wiki/Rick_Adelman" title="Rick Adelman">Rick Adelman</a> announced his retirement from coaching.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On April 21, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Utah_Jazz" title="Utah Jazz">Utah Jazz</a> opted not to renew head coach <a href="/wiki/Tyrone_Corbin" title="Tyrone Corbin">Tyrone Corbin</a>'s contract after over three full seasons with the team.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On April 30, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a> head coach <a href="/wiki/Mike_D%27Antoni" title="Mike D'Antoni">Mike D'Antoni</a> resigned after nearly two years with the team.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On May 6, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a> fired head coach <a href="/wiki/Mark_Jackson_(basketball)" title="Mark Jackson (basketball)">Mark Jackson</a> after three years with the team.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On May 12, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> fired head coach <a href="/wiki/Mike_Brown_(basketball,_born_1970)" title="Mike Brown (basketball, born 1970)">Mike Brown</a> after one year of the second tenure with the team.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On May 14, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Pistons" title="Detroit Pistons">Detroit Pistons</a> hired <a href="/wiki/Stan_Van_Gundy" title="Stan Van Gundy">Stan Van Gundy</a> as the head coach.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On May 19, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a> hired <a href="/wiki/Steve_Kerr" title="Steve Kerr">Steve Kerr</a> as head coach.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On June 5, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Minnesota Timberwolves</a> announced that <a href="/wiki/Flip_Saunders" title="Flip Saunders">Flip Saunders</a> will return to the Timberwolves as head coach.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On June 6, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Utah_Jazz" title="Utah Jazz">Utah Jazz</a> hired <a href="/wiki/Quin_Snyder" title="Quin Snyder">Quin Snyder</a>, formerly an assistant with the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a>, as head coach.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On June 10, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a> hired <a href="/wiki/Derek_Fisher" title="Derek Fisher">Derek Fisher</a> as head coach.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On June 20, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> hired <a href="/wiki/David_Blatt" title="David Blatt">David Blatt</a> as head coach.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On June 30, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a> traded their two future second picks to the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Nets" title="Brooklyn Nets">Brooklyn Nets</a> for their head coach <a href="/wiki/Jason_Kidd" title="Jason Kidd">Jason Kidd</a> and fired head coach <a href="/wiki/Larry_Drew" title="Larry Drew">Larry Drew</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On July 2, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Nets" title="Brooklyn Nets">Brooklyn Nets</a> hired <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Hollins" title="Lionel Hollins">Lionel Hollins</a> as head coach.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On July 29, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a> hired <a href="/wiki/Byron_Scott" title="Byron Scott">Byron Scott</a> as head coach.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In-Season">In-Season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In-Season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>On December 15, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_Kings" title="Sacramento Kings">Sacramento Kings</a> fired <a href="/wiki/Mike_Malone_(basketball)" title="Mike Malone (basketball)" class="mw-redirect">Mike Malone</a> of his head coaching duties and replaced him with <a href="/wiki/Tyrone_Corbin" title="Tyrone Corbin">Tyrone Corbin</a> on an interim basis.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preseason">Preseason</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Preseason">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The preseason began on Saturday, October 4, 2014 and ended on Friday, October 24, 2014.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regular_season">Regular season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Regular season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The regular season began on Tuesday, October 28, 2014, with the defending NBA champions <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Spurs" title="San Antonio Spurs">San Antonio Spurs</a> hosted the first game against the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> The regular season will end on Wednesday, April 15, 2015. <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association_Christmas_games" title="National Basketball Association Christmas games">Christmas Day</a> games will be played on Thursday, December 25, 2014. The NBA is mulling a week-long <a href="/wiki/NBA_All-Star_Game" title="NBA All-Star Game">All-Star break</a> in February 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> and the full schedule was released during a one-hour schedule release special on August 13, 2014 at 6:00&#160;p.m. <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">EDT</a> on <a href="/wiki/NBA_TV" title="NBA TV">NBA TV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playoffs">Playoffs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014%E2%80%9315_NBA_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Playoffs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2015 NBA playoffs will begin on Saturday, April 18, 2015 and will conclude with the <a href="/wiki/2015_NBA_Finals" title="2015 NBA Finals" class="mw-redirect">2015 NBA Finals</a> which will begin on June 4, 2015 on ABC. <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> will broadcast the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Conference_Finals" title="NBA Conference Finals">Western Conference Finals</a> and <a href="/wiki/TNT_(TV_channel)" title="TNT (TV channel)">TNT</a> will broadcast the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Conference_Finals" title="NBA Conference Finals">Eastern Conference Finals</a>. ABC will broadcast the NBA Finals.</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/LeBron_James" title="LeBron James">LeBron James</a> returned to his home state of <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> after four years with the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Heat" title="Miami Heat">Miami Heat</a>. James controversially announced his departure from Cleveland in a nationally televised special - <a href="/wiki/The_Decision_(TV_special)" title="The Decision (TV special)">The Decision</a> - on July 8, 2010.</li>
<li>The Charlotte Bobcats were renamed the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Hornets" title="Charlotte Hornets">Charlotte Hornets</a> on May 20, 2014 to begin the 2014–15 season.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> The newly established Hornets retain the 10 year history of the Bobcats, as well as regain the original Charlotte Hornets team records from the <a href="/wiki/1988%E2%80%9389_NBA_season" title="1988–89 NBA season">1988–89 NBA season</a> through the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_NBA_season" title="2001–02 NBA season">2001–02 NBA season</a>. The <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans_Pelicans" title="New Orleans Pelicans">New Orleans Pelicans</a> retain the remaining history that exists under the New Orleans Hornets name from the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_NBA_season" title="2002–03 NBA season">2002–03 NBA season</a> through the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_NBA_season" title="2012–13 NBA season">2012–13 NBA season</a> including the New Orleans/Oklahoma City Hornets name from the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_NBA_season" title="2005–06 NBA season">2005–06 NBA season</a> through the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_NBA_season" title="2006–07 NBA season">2006–07 NBA season</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> won the #1 pick in the NBA draft for the second year in a row, as well as the third time in four years and the sixth time in franchise history. It also ties the lowest percentage possible in the modern-day draft lottery with the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bulls" title="Chicago Bulls">Chicago Bulls</a> back in <a href="/wiki/2008_NBA_draft" title="2008 NBA draft">2008</a>, as well as marks the second time one team got the #1 draft pick for the second year in a row. Their selection was <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Wiggins" title="Andrew Wiggins">Andrew Wiggins</a>, the second straight <a href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian" class="mw-redirect">Canadian</a> player to be selected as the No. 1 pick.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">Houston Rockets</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Minnesota Timberwolves</a> played at the <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City_Arena" title="Mexico City Arena">Mexico City Arena</a> on November 12, 2014, while the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a> will play at <a href="/wiki/The_O2_Arena" title="The O2 Arena">The O2 Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> on January 15, 2015. The <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Raptors" title="Toronto Raptors">Toronto Raptors</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a> will play at the <a href="/wiki/Bell_Centre" title="Bell Centre">Bell Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> on October 24, 2014 and the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Raptors" title="Toronto Raptors">Toronto Raptors</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_Kings" title="Sacramento Kings">Sacramento Kings</a> played at the <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Arena" title="Rogers Arena">Rogers Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a> on October 5, 2014 during the pre-season.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Raptors" title="Toronto Raptors">Toronto Raptors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Grizzlies" title="Memphis Grizzlies">Memphis Grizzlies</a> will celebrate their 20th anniversaries as franchises in the <a href="/wiki/NBA" title="NBA" class="mw-redirect">NBA</a>, although the latter was formerly known as the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Grizzlies" title="Vancouver Grizzlies">Vancouver Grizzlies</a>.</li>
<li>Both top selections Andrew Wiggins and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Bennett_(basketball)" title="Anthony Bennett (basketball)">Anthony Bennett</a> were traded to the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Minnesota Timberwolves</a> for <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Love" title="Kevin Love">Kevin Love</a> on <a href="/wiki/August_23rd" title="August 23rd" class="mw-redirect">August 23rd, 2014</a>, the soonest date allowed by Wiggins signing his rookie contract 30 days earlier. The trade of Wiggins also marked the second time since the <a href="/wiki/NBA-ABA_merger" title="NBA-ABA merger" class="mw-redirect">NBA-ABA merger</a> that a #1 draft pick would be traded to another team without playing for the team he was initially drafted by.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Spurs" title="San Antonio Spurs">San Antonio Spurs</a> made history by hiring <a href="/wiki/Becky_Hammon" title="Becky Hammon">Becky Hammon</a> as the first full-time female assistant coach in any of the <a href="/wiki/Major_professional_sports_leagues_in_the_United_States_and_Canada" title="Major professional sports leagues in the United States and Canada">four major U.S. professional sports leagues</a>. Hammon, a guard for the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_National_Basketball_Association" title="Women's National Basketball Association">WNBA</a>'s <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Stars" title="San Antonio Stars">San Antonio Stars</a>, joined the Spurs coaching staff upon her retirement at the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/2014_WNBA_season" title="2014 WNBA season">2014 WNBA season</a>.</li>
<li>During the month of September, <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a> co-owner Bruce Levenson would end up selling his share of the Hawks after self-reporting an e-mail he received that was considered inappropriate and potentially racist back in 2012. Around that same time, general manager <a href="/wiki/Danny_Ferry" title="Danny Ferry">Danny Ferry</a> would leak out remarks that player <a href="/wiki/Luol_Deng" title="Luol Deng">Luol Deng</a> "had a bit of African in him" from a transcript he received, which would eventually result in Ferry taking an indefinite leave of absence and having coach <a href="/wiki/Mike_Budenholzer" title="Mike Budenholzer">Mike Budenholzer</a> taking on the job until his potential return.</li>
<li>On September 29, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a> became the first team to have two different sets of brothers playing on the same team, with <a href="/wiki/Goran_Dragi%C4%87" title="Goran Dragić">Goran Dragić</a>'s younger brother <a href="/wiki/Zoran_Dragi%C4%87" title="Zoran Dragić">Zoran</a> signing on a two year deal and both twin brothers <a href="/wiki/Markieff_Morris" title="Markieff Morris">Markieff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Morris_(basketball)" title="Marcus Morris (basketball)">Marcus Morris</a> both getting four year contract extensions from the team. All four of these players would first end up playing in the same game in a loss to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a> on November 15, 2014, while both pairs of brothers would end up playing together at the same time late in the fourth quarter in a 112-96 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a> on January 2, 2015.</li>
<li>On October 6, 2014, the NBA announced a new TV deal with <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> and <a href="/wiki/TNT_(TV_channel)" title="TNT (TV channel)">TNT</a> that's worth $24 billion on a 9 year deal. Starting in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_NBA_season" title="2015–16 NBA season" class="mw-redirect">2015–16 NBA season</a>, the NBA's value would go from $930 million to $2.6 billion per year, which would also increase each team's salary cap from $63 million to around $88.8 million. In addition to that, the NBA also announced that starting this season, the league will host an end-of-season award show on TNT to replace the time spent on giving out certain awards in sometimes awkward situations.</li>
<li>On October 19, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Nets" title="Brooklyn Nets">Brooklyn Nets</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a> played a pre-season game that consisted of four 11 minute quarters, instead of the regular 12 minute quarters, which decreased the overall amount of playing time to around two hours. Boston won the game 95-90.</li>
<li>The NBA logo assumes a new position on team jerseys, moving from the top left chest in front to the back atop a player's name.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> In addition, teams who have won a championship will have their uniforms adorned with a gold patch on the back, featuring the number of championships won.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>In spite of a 17–13 majority voting for a new lottery reform that would have taken effect immediately, the NBA declines the opportunity to change the lottery that makes the worst 4 teams hold equal odds for the #1 pick and gives middling teams a better chance at a Top 6 pick.</li>
<li>The NBA also implements and debuts a replay center in <a href="/wiki/Secaucus,_New_Jersey" title="Secaucus, New Jersey">Secaucus, New Jersey</a> on October 28, 2014. Its purpose is to make sure the NBA's replays on questionable calls faster for the referees when making the final decision.</li>
<li>For the second time this season, the league again experimented on-court <a href="/wiki/Advertising" title="Advertising">advertising</a>. The <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knicks" title="New York Knicks">New York Knicks</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Heat" title="Miami Heat">Miami Heat</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a>, which had sponsorship with <a href="/wiki/Chase_(bank)" title="Chase (bank)">Chase</a> for the Knicks, <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Electronics" title="Samsung Electronics">Samsung Electronics</a> for the Heat and Warriors and <a href="/wiki/Bwin.Party_Digital_Entertainment" title="Bwin.Party Digital Entertainment">PartyPoker.com</a> for Sixers are now removed and now replaced with the website and/or <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> handles of the team, while the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Pacers" title="Indiana Pacers">Indiana Pacers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Raptors" title="Toronto Raptors">Toronto Raptors</a> retain marquee sponsorship with the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Economic_Development_Corporation" title="Indiana Economic Development Corporation">Indiana Economic Development Corporation</a> for the Pacers and <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Montreal" title="Bank of Montreal">Bank of Montreal</a> for the Raptors respectively. Meanwhile, additional teams join in the experiment. The <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">Houston Rockets</a> court at the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Center" title="Toyota Center">Toyota Center</a> will be sponsored by <a href="/wiki/ZTE" title="ZTE">ZTE</a> to promote its smartphones.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Magic" title="Orlando Magic">Orlando Magic</a> court at <a href="/wiki/Amway_Center" title="Amway Center">Amway Center</a> will be sponsored by <a href="/wiki/FanDuel" title="FanDuel">FanDuel</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> while the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Nets" title="Brooklyn Nets">Brooklyn Nets</a> court at <a href="/wiki/Barclays_Center" title="Barclays Center">Barclays Center</a> will be sponsored by AirFastTickets, a ticketing company based in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Hornets" title="Charlotte Hornets">Charlotte Hornets</a> court at the <a href="/wiki/Time_Warner_Cable_Arena" title="Time Warner Cable Arena">Time Warner Cable Arena</a> will be sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Novant_Health" title="Novant Health">Novant Health</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Utah_Jazz" title="Utah Jazz">Utah Jazz</a>'s court at the <a href="/wiki/EnergySolutions_Arena" title="EnergySolutions Arena">EnergySolutions Arena</a> will be sponsored by United Fuel Supply, a oil company based in <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a> and established in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>On November 1, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Carmelo_Anthony" title="Carmelo Anthony">Carmelo Anthony</a> surpassed 20,000 career points, becoming the 40th player in NBA history to do so. He reached the milestone in the 1st quarter of a 96-93 home win against the <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Hornets" title="Charlotte Hornets">Charlotte Hornets</a>.</li>
<li>On November 11, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Nowitzki" title="Dirk Nowitzki">Dirk Nowitzki</a> surpassed <a href="/wiki/Hakeem_Olajuwon" title="Hakeem Olajuwon">Hakeem Olajuwon</a> as the highest-scoring player born outside the United States and the 9th all-time scoring leader, as the Mavericks came from behind 24 points down to beat Sacramento 106–98 for their 21st straight regular-season win at home against the Kings. Nowitzki hit a jumper from just inside the three-point line early in the fourth quarter to pass Olajuwon at No. 9, and he finished the night at 26,953 career points.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>On November 12, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Kobe_Bryant" title="Kobe Bryant">Kobe Bryant</a> surpassed <a href="/wiki/John_Havlicek" title="John Havlicek">John Havlicek</a> and became the all-time leader for missed field goal attempts in the league. His career-breaking record 13,418th missed shot came at 6:22 in the fourth quarter with a missed 14-foot fadeaway in a 107-102 loss on the road against the <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Grizzlies" title="Memphis Grizzlies">Memphis Grizzlies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>On November 13, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a> were leading by 44 at halftime over the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a>, tying for the 2nd-highest leading margin in NBA History at halftime.</li>
<li>On December 2, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a> would announce that as of the end of this season, the venue that they'd play under (the <a href="/wiki/US_Airways_Center" title="US Airways Center" class="mw-redirect">US Airways Center</a>) would be renamed as the <a href="/wiki/Talking_Stick_Resort_Arena" title="Talking Stick Resort Arena">Talking Stick Resort Arena</a>.</li>
<li>On December 3, 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Minnesota Timberwolves</a> would create the first ever <a href="/wiki/Mulligan" title="Mulligan" class="mw-disambig">mulligan</a> during a start of an official NBA game by removing the first 16 seconds where the 76ers gained possession and drew a foul against the Timberwolves. The 76ers would also gain their first victory of the season in that game.</li>
<li>On December 13, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Howard" title="Dwight Howard">Dwight Howard</a> reached 10,000 career rebounds.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>On December 14, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Kobe_Bryant" title="Kobe Bryant">Kobe Bryant</a> surpassed <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jordan" title="Michael Jordan">Michael Jordan</a> for third place on the all-time scoring list. He'd reach that mark at 5:24 in the second quarter in a road game against the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Minnesota Timberwolves</a> by making two free-throw shots. Kobe would score 9 points to surpass him and a total of 26 points in the Lakers' 100-94 victory over Minnesota.</li>
<li>On December 18, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Rajon_Rondo" title="Rajon Rondo">Rajon Rondo</a> and rookie <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Powell" title="Dwight Powell">Dwight Powell</a> would be traded to the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a> in exchange for the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a> receiving <a href="/wiki/Brandan_Wright" title="Brandan Wright">Brandan Wright</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jae_Crowder" title="Jae Crowder">Jae Crowder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jameer_Nelson" title="Jameer Nelson">Jameer Nelson</a>, a 2015 first round draft pick, and a future second round draft pick.</li>
<li>On December 22, 2014, the Detroit Pistons waived <a href="/wiki/Josh_Smith" title="Josh Smith">Josh Smith</a>, despite signing him to a four-year, $54 million contract in July 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> Smith would end up signing with the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">Houston Rockets</a> two days later in exchange for the removal of undrafted rookie <a href="/wiki/Tarik_Black" title="Tarik Black">Tarik Black</a>. In Smith 's debut with the Rockets, he'd record 21 points and 8 rebounds in 32 minutes under a 117–111 overtime victory over the <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Grizzlies" title="Memphis Grizzlies">Memphis Grizzlies</a> on December 26, 2014.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Miami_Heat" title="Miami Heat">Miami Heat</a> defeated the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> 101–91 on Christmas Day, spoiling <a href="/wiki/LeBron_James" title="LeBron James">LeBron James</a>' return to Miami, where he previously led the Heat to the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Finals" title="NBA Finals">NBA Finals</a> in each of his four seasons, winning two of them in 2012 and 2013.</li>
<li>On December 26, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Nowitzki" title="Dirk Nowitzki">Dirk Nowitzki</a> would surpass <a href="/wiki/Elvin_Hayes" title="Elvin Hayes">Elvin Hayes</a> to become the 8th best all-time scorer in the NBA. He'd score 14 points in the Mavericks' close 102–98 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a>. During that same day, <a href="/wiki/Jared_Dudley" title="Jared Dudley">Jared Dudley</a> would be the first ever player to be perfect while shooting at least 10 baskets and 3 three-point shots as he'd score 24 points off of 10/10 shooting (4/4 from the three-point line) in the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a>' 107-77 blowout victory over the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a>.</li>
<li>On January 2, 2015, an agreement was put in place by the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a> ownership to sell the franchise.</li>
</ul>
